# Knowledge Base (internalized)
## Hormozi Copy Principles
- The hook is a promise. The body is the proof. The CTA is the path.
- Specificity = credibility. Vague copy loses. "Lost 12 kg" beats "lost weight".
- The job of each line: make the reader read the next line.

## יצחק's 9-Part Ad Structure
Apply sequentially within the body of each variant:
1. **Pattern Interrupt** — break the scroll with something unexpected
2. **Connection** (חיבור) — "אני מכיר את התחושה" — instantly mirror the reader's reality
3. **Story** (סיפור) — a brief personal or relatable story to build trust
4. **Problem** (בעיה) — name the core pain explicitly, in the avatar's language
5. **Deepening** (העמקה) — amplify the consequence of staying stuck
6. **General Solution** (פתרון כללי) — acknowledge that solutions exist
7. **My Solution** (פתרון שלי) — reveal the product as the specific, superior path
8. **CTA** — clear, single action step
9. **Scarcity** — real urgency (time/quantity/access); never fabricated

## 7 Crack Post Parameters (quality checklist per variant)
Every strong ad hits most of these:
1. Problem/Pain — is the pain clearly named?
2. Solution — is the solution clearly presented?
3. Urgency/Criticality — why act now?
4. Agenda Proof — why should they trust YOU?
5. Benefit + Amplified Benefit — stated benefit + what that benefit enables
6. Belief it will work — mechanism, proof, or social proof
7. CTA — single, frictionless action

## 3 USP Templates (pick the closest fit)
- **Mechanism**: "The only [product] that [unique mechanism] so you can [outcome]"
- **Contrast**: "Unlike [alternatives], [product] [does X differently]"
- **Avatar-specific**: "[Product] built specifically for [exact avatar] who [specific situation]"

## 3 Avatar Layers (write to all three simultaneously)
- **External** (what they want): the surface goal (lose weight, make money, save time)
- **Deep** (why they want it): the emotional driver behind the goal (confidence, freedom, respect)
- **Base** (identity/values): who they want to become or stop being ("I want to be the kind of person who…")

## 8 Headline Types (use one per variant)
1. Direct benefit: "Get [outcome] in [time]"
2. Curiosity gap: "The [X] most [audience] don't know about"
3. News: "Introducing the [new thing] that [does X]"
4. How-to: "How to [achieve outcome] without [common obstacle]"
5. Question: "Are you still [doing the old way]?"
6. Proof: "[Specific number] [audience] already [result]"
7. Command: "[Verb] your [desired outcome] today"
8. Us vs Them: "Why [audience] choose [us] over [alternative]"
